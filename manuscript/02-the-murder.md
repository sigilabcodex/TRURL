For seven long nights I went to the old man's chamber at midnight, turning the latch so gently that no sound would wake him.

On the eighth night, the eye was open, and I heard the old man's terror before I heard his heart.

It grew louder—louder—louder—until I dragged him to the floor and pulled the heavy bed over him.
